Note to screen reader users: this tutorial is a simulated click-through demonstration. On each page, a screenshot image with a button hotspot shows you where to click to move to the next slide. Once you go to the new slide, you can move the screen reader focus to the slide title by moving back or up one. If you move the screen reader focus forward, you will be past the slide and can access the lower navigation controls to skip through the tutorial to different sections. This tutorial covers three database interfaces: PubMed, Google Scholar, and ProQuest.

Google Scholar (GS) Search

With your search term in the search box, click the "Search" magnifying glass button to start your search.

Click the Search magnifying glass icon to search for articles in Google Scholar.

GS: Results List

Many articles listed in the results will have a Cited by link under the article description.

Google Scholar also includes the number of articles that have cited it, which is one indication of how influential the article is.

Click 'Cited by' to see the list of articles.

GS Cited By List

The full list of articles may be very long (this one is 8,430) so you may also want to narrow down that list using some keywords.

Click the "Search within citing articles" checkbox to narrow your results.

GS: Search Within

The full list of articles may be very long (this one is 8,430) so you may also want to add more search terms to narrow down that list.

Be sure the check the "Search within citing articles" option to avoid starting a whole new search.

Click the Search magnifying glass icon to narrow your results.

GS: Search Within

Now there are fewer articles in the list, but adding more keywords or limiting results by date range will help narrow it down even further.
